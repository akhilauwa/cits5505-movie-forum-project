Cabrini

"After witnessing disease and poverty in the slums of New York, Italian immigrant Francesca Cabrini embarks on a daring journey to persuade the hostile mayor to provide housing and healthcare for hundreds of orphaned children."

Director: Alejandro Monteverde


Writers: Rod BarrAlejandro Monteverde


Stars: Cristiana Dell'AnnaJohn LithgowDavid Morse



Movie Introduction

In 1889 New York, Italian immigrant Paolo and Mother Frances Xavier Cabrini, a nun, work to aid poor Italian immigrants. Cabrini, denied the founding of her missionary order in Italy, is advised by the Pope to move to New York instead. There, she faces hostility but establishes charity work and a children’s home despite opposition from local authorities and the mafia. With the help of a local doctor and funding from various immigrant communities, Cabrini plans a hospital. Despite challenges, including racial tensions and an arson attack, her perseverance leads to the eventual construction of the hospital. Cabrini’s efforts are widely recognized, and she is later canonized as the first American saint, patron of immigrants.
